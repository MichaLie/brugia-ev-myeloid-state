# Main Figure Legends

## Figure 1. Reproducible frozen Core EV-response signature derived from preserved monocyte and moDC datasets.

**(A)** Gene-level log2 fold changes for EV versus media exposure in monocytes and monocyte-derived dendritic cells (moDCs). Grey points indicate all shared evaluable genes. Blue points indicate genes retained in the frozen down arm, and vermillion points indicate genes retained in the frozen up arm. **(B)** Composition of the final frozen 76-gene signature, showing 56 down-regulated and 20 up-regulated genes. **(C)** Consensus Hallmark enrichment structure across the two derivation datasets. Values are normalized enrichment scores (NES) from preranked Hallmark analysis, with positive values indicating enrichment among EV-up genes and negative values indicating enrichment among EV-down genes. Together, these panels define the compact, cross-dataset state used in all downstream analyses.

## Figure 2. Two-arm regulatory architecture of the reconstructed EV-response state.

**(A)** Prespecified transcription-factor enrichment across the frozen signature and its down and up arms. Point position indicates the best enrichment false-discovery rate across the frozen libraries; larger points indicate signal recovered across more libraries. **(B)** Fractional coverage of the down arm and deterministic modules by frozen SPI1 and RUNX1 union regulons. **(C)** Deterministic module sizes for Module A, Module B, and Module C. **(D)** Alignment between the preserved EV-protein ortholog score and the reconstructed host-state scores in the baseline myeloid atlas. The central pattern is an asymmetric architecture in which the down arm sits in a SPI1/RUNX1-linked myeloid-state neighborhood, whereas the up arm is dominated by MYC/MAX-associated structure.

## Figure 3. Canonical seed-based miRNA targeting is insufficient to explain the global down arm.

**(A)** Direct-target rerun for the preserved 17-gene regulator panel in TargetScan and miRDB using hsa-miR-100-5p as the human surrogate query. No panel gene was recovered as a direct hit in either preserved resource. **(B)** Observed-to-expected ratios for canonical parasite-derived 6-mer motifs in the down-arm 3'UTRs. All four tested 6-mers were depleted. **(C)** Observed-to-expected ratios for the corresponding 7-mer motifs, which showed no positive enrichment signal. The positive-control note indicates that `MTOR` remained a predicted hsa-miR-100-5p target in both preserved resources, showing that target-space support for a limited mTOR-linked interaction was retained even though the broader panel-level model was not.

## Figure 4. TLR-context and orthogonal perturbation analyses resolve a two-axis host-state model.

**(A)** Directional concordance fractions for the innate-like axis and the MYC-associated axis across acute TLR stimulation and orthogonal perturbation contexts. The innate-like axis is represented by the down arm or Module A, and the MYC-associated axis by the up arm or Module B, depending on the contrast. Acute TLR and LPS stimulation preferentially reconstruct the innate-like axis, whereas temsirolimus in differentiating moDCs reconstructs both axes. **(B)** Mean donor-paired change in the Module B EV-like score across orthogonal perturbations. Negative values indicate movement away from the EV-like MYC-associated arm; positive values indicate movement toward it. Exact paired sign-flip *P* values are shown above bars. **(C)** Two-axis state-space summarizing innate-like-axis concordance on the x-axis and MYC-associated-axis concordance on the y-axis across TLR, orthogonal-validation, and external transport contexts. The plot highlights the separation between innate-concordant and MYC-associated components of the reconstructed EV-response state.

## Figure 5. External transport defines boundary conditions for the reconstructed state and supports a bounded composite-state model.

**(A)** Total gene-level concordance fractions across all external contrasts. Colors indicate context group. No contrast met the pipeline's formal supportive threshold. **(B)** Context-level directional concordance for the down and up arms across direct *Brugia* exposure, natural infection, a same-parasite stress test, and cross-helminth perturbation settings. The down arm transports more stably than the up arm in most contexts outside selected LPS-background settings. **(C)** Composite-state model summarizing the reconstructed EV-response state. The down arm is most consistent with an innate-concordant, SPI1/RUNX1-linked contraction of a myeloid program, whereas the up arm is a context-dependent MYC-associated biosynthetic program. Canonical seed-based miRNA targeting remains insufficient to explain the full state, and external datasets define transport boundaries rather than uniform confirmation.
